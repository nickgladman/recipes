### Creamy Chicken and Rice Casserole

**originally from: https://www.thekitchn.com/chicken-rice-casserole-recipe-256188#post-recipe-12572**

**modified by Nick**

## Ingredients

Cooking spray

3 tablespoons unsalted butter

1 cup diced red onion (from 1 large onion)

3 tablespoons all-purpose flour

800-1000 ml low-sodium chicken broth (I use low sodium Better Than Bouillon)

1/2 cup whole or 2% milk (adjust as needed)

3/4-1 cup uncooked long-grain white rice

3 cups frozen mixed vegetables, such as carrots, corn, peas, and green beans

680-900 grams (1 1/2-2 pounds) boneless, skinless chicken thighs, trimmed of excess fat. Cut into small cubes (~2 cm or smaller)

1/2-1 cup shredded cheddar cheese (to desired cheesiness)

Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Kosher salt, to taste

## Instructions

1. Before preheating, season chicken with salt and msg and chill in the fridge for a minimum of 30 minutes.

2. Heat the oven and prepare the baking dish. Arrange a rack in the middle of the oven and heat the oven to 375°F. Coat an 11x7-inch or 1 1/2-quart baking dish with cooking spray; set aside.

3. Sauté the onion in butter. Melt 3 heaping tablespoons unsalted butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add 1 cup diced yellow onion and 1 1/2 teaspoons of the kosher salt and sauté until onion is soft but not browned, 3 to 5 minutes.

4. Stir in the flour. Sprinkle in 3 heaping tablespoons all-purpose flour and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly to coat the flour in the butter and to cook off the raw flour flavor.

5. Add the chicken broth and milk and cook until thickened. Pour in 1 3/4 cups low-sodium chicken broth and whisk to incorporate, breaking up any lumps of flour and onion and scraping browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Pour in 1/2 cup whole or 2% milk and 1/4 teaspoon of the black pepper and whisk to completely incorporate. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly, until sauce thickens, about 3 minutes.

6. Add sauce to rice and vegetables in baking dish. Place 3/4 cup uncooked long-grain white rice and 3 cups frozen mixed vegetables in the prepared baking dish. Pour in the sauce and stir to combine.

7. Distrute and nestle the chicken cubes on top of the rice mixture--try to submerge them at least partially in the gravy. Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil.

8. Bake for 1 hour. If cooking the chicken whole, bake until the rice is tender and an instant-read thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the chicken registers 160°F. Generally one hour will be sufficient.

9. Turn on the broiler. Uncover the casserole and top with shredded cheese in an even layer. Return, uncovered, under broiler and gratinate to desired crispiness.
