### Ranginak Persian Date Cake (Halva)

**Modified by Nick**

* Originally from: https://www.washingtonpost.com/recipes/persian-date-cake-ranginak/

### Ingredients

* 1 cup coarsely chopped walnuts
	* to to sift out any small particles or dust since they will burn easily when roasting

* 3 packed cups pitted dates, preferably deglet noor (about 1 pound). Adjust as needed to your pan size
	* I just pitted some Medjool dates. Buying already pitted is a nice time saver

* 32 tablespoons of high fat butter (Kerrygold or similar. I have not tried ghee yet)
	* Original recipe called for 2 cup safflower oil

* 2.5-4 cups flour for 32 tablespoons butter
	* Note: needed more like 2.5-3 cups to ensure enough coverage in my springform pan
	* you can use 2.5 cups for 32 tablespoons of butter, but the roux will be very liquid still and need to cool and then sit in the fridge overnight before it firms up enough to remove from the springform pan.

* 5 tablespoons confectioners' sugar

* 2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

* 5 teaspoons ground cardamom
	* 4 teaspoons was what I used previously and it made a nice flavor, but I think it could stand to have more cardamom

* 2 cup ground raw pistachios (or enough to cover the top of the pan)
	* get lightly salted or unsalted pistachios (NOT ROASTED!) and blitz in food processor.

* 9 inch non-stick springform pan



Instructions:

1. Toast the walnuts in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat for about 5 minutes, shaking the pan so they don’t scorch, until the nuts are fragrant and lightly browned. Let cool.
	* Note: sift out any small particles or dust since they will burn easily when roasting

2. Line the bottom of a springform pan or ~10-inch tart pan with a removable bottom or a springform pan with parchment paper.
	* Note: I found for the non-stick springform pan, the ranginak pealed off easily after it sat overnight in the fridge, so parchment paper may not be necessary (it's also a hassle to peal off of the ranginak if it gets into crevices).

3. Use the walnuts to stuff each date, arranging them in the pan in a single layer as you work. Press the dates down (smoosh them) to create as much of a solid layer as possible so that the roux will mostly sit on top.

4. Mix together the cardamom and cinnamon before starting the roux (technically this will be a halva).

5. Heat the butter in the same skillet you used to toast the walnuts, over medium heat. Gradually add the flour, whisking constantly, to form a smooth and thick roux. Cook for 15 to 20 minutes, whisking all the while, until the roux is a light mocha color; it picks up color especially in the last 5 minutes of cooking. Turn off the heat and stir in the sugar and then the cardomom and cinnamon, until it has dissolved and is well mixed--it's important to keep stirring since the spices can burn easily on the bottom of the pan.
	* Note: the roux can thicken a lot near the end of cooking and it needs to be thicker so that it forms a solid dough around the dates
		* Optional approach: You can use less flour for this roux (2.5 cups for 32 tablespoons) and it will thicken after sitting in the fridge overnight, but I would err on adding a bit more flour (I ended up using 2.5 cups of flour in 2026 and it turned out fine)
	
6. Carefully (the halva is molten hot!) transfer the halva to the pan; use an offset spatula or other utensile to smooth it evenly over the dates, making sure they are completely covered. Immediately sprinkle with ground pistachios until uniformly covered and no roux is visible 	
	* if using a roux/halva with less flour, more of the pistachios will sink into it than with a thicker halva--just add pistachio until you can see a layer that is not getting wet from the halva.

7. Let the date cake sit until room temp (will take 3-4 hours). If making a roux with less flour (~2.5 cups per 32 tablespoons butter), you will need to transfer the pan to the fridge (well covered) overnight to let the halva set completely. The cake will easily pop off of a non-stick springform pan bottom.

8. When ready to serve, remove the ring from the pan. Cut the cake into squares of diagonals (edges and bits are for the cook).
