### Ranginak Persian Date Cake

**Modified by Nick**

* Originally from: https://www.washingtonpost.com/recipes/persian-date-cake-ranginak/

### Ingredients

* 1 cup coarsely chopped walnuts
	* to to sift out any small particles or dust since they will burn easily when roasting

* 3 packed cups pitted dates, preferably deglet noor (about 1 pound; see headnote)
	* I just pitted some Medjool dates, but maybe a smaller date might be nicer since it'll be more bite-sized. It turned out well though using medjool


* 32 tablespoons of high fat butter (Kerrygold or similar)
	* 1 cup safflower oil (may substitute 16 tablespoons unsalted butter or ghee)
	* need more like 20-24 tablespoons butter, use Kerrygold butter since it has higher fat compared to the Kirklands brand. The one bit of feedback I got was the roux needed more butter, so springing for ghee might be the way to go.
	* for larger roux amount use 32 tablespoons (2 cups) of butter

* 2.5-4 cups flour for 32 tablespoons butter
	* Note: needed more like 2.5-3 cups to ensure enough coverage in my springform pan
	* you can use 2.5 cups for 32 tablespoons of butter, but the roux will be very liquid still and need to cool and then sit in the fridge overnight before it firms up enough to remove from the springform pan.

* 5 tablespoons confectioners' sugar

* 2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

* 5 teaspoons ground cardamom
	* 4 teaspoons was what I used previously and it made a nice flavor, but I think it could stand to have more cardamom

* 2 cup ground raw pistachios
	* get lightly salted or unsalted pistachios (NOT ROASTED!) and blitz in food processor.

* Springform pan



Instructions:

1. Toast the walnuts in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat for about 5 minutes, shaking the pan so they don’t scorch, until the nuts are fragrant and lightly browned. Let cool.
	* Note: to to sift out any small particles or dust since they will burn easily when roasting

2. Line the bottom of a springform pan or ~10-inch tart pan with a removable bottom or a springform pan with parchment paper.

3. Use the walnuts to stuff each date, arranging them in the pan in a single layer as you work; this will take 15 to 20 minutes, and it should be easy to do because the dates have been split. Press the dates down (smoosh them) to create as much of a solid layer as possible so that the roux will mostly sit on top. Mix together the cardamom and cinnamon before starting the roux.

4. Heat the butter in the same skillet you used to toast the walnuts, over medium heat. Gradually add the flour, whisking constantly, to form a smooth and thick roux. Cook for 15 to 20 minutes, whisking all the while, until the roux is a light mocha color; it picks up color especially in the last 5 minutes of cooking. Turn off the heat and stir in the sugar and then the cardomom and cinnamon, until it has dissolved and is well mixed--it's important to keep stirring since the spices can burn easily on the bottom of the pan.
	* remember: it needs to be thicker so that it forms a solid dough around the dates
		* You can use less flour for this roux (2.5 cups for 32 tablespoons) and it will thicken after sitting in the fridge overnight, but I would err on adding a bit more flour (this is what I ended up making in 2026 and it turned out fine)
	* the roux can thicken a lot near the end, so add the sugar earlier than you think so it doesn't seize up the roux

5. Carefully (the roux is molten hot!) transfer the roux to the pan; use an offset spatula or table knife to smooth it evenly over the dates, making sure they are completely covered. Immediately sprinkle with ground pistachios until uniformly covered and no roux is visible 	
	* if using a roux with less flour, more of the pistachios will sink into it than with a thicker roux--just add pistachio until you can see a layer that is not getting wet from the roux.

6. Let the date cake sit until room temp (will take 3-4 hours). If making a roux with less flour (~2.5 cups per 32 tablespoons butter), you will need to transfer the pan to the fridge (well covered) overnight to let the halva set completely. The cake will easily pop off of a non-stick springform pan bottom.

7. When ready to serve, remove the ring from the pan. Cut the cake into 18 to 24 squares (edges and bits are for the cook).
